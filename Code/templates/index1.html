Alzhimer Disease Stages

Non Demented

Non-Demented refers to individuals who do not exhibit any significant signs or symptoms of dementia. This group typically includes people with normal cognitive function or those who may experience mild age-related cognitive changes that are not severe enough to interfere with daily life.

Read More

Very Mild Demented

Very Mild Dementia is an early stage of dementia where individuals begin to experience noticeable but minor cognitive decline. It is often challenging to diagnose as the symptoms can be subtle and may overlap with normal age-related memory issues. However, identifying this stage early can help in implementing interventions to slow progression and improve quality of life.

Read More

Mild Demented

Mild Demented refers to an early stage of dementia, where cognitive decline begins to interfere with a person's daily life but is not yet severe. In this stage, individuals may experience noticeable memory problems and difficulty with complex tasks, though they can still maintain a relatively high level of independence.

Read More

Moderate Demented

Moderate Demented refers to a middle stage of dementia, which is a progressive neurological condition characterized by a decline in cognitive function, memory, and the ability to perform daily activities. This stage is typically associated with moderate impairment in cognitive and functional abilities, and it represents a progression from the earlier mild stage of dementia.
